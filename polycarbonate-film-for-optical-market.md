<p><p>Polycarbonate Film for Optical Market Types refers to the categorization of the film used in the optical industry. Optically Isotropic Film is a type of polycarbonate film that exhibits the same optical properties in all directions, making it useful for applications like displays and lenses that require uniform light transmission. On the other hand, Optically Anisotropic Film is a type of polycarbonate film that has different optical properties depending on the direction of light passing through it. It is commonly used in applications such as polarizers and wave plates that manipulate light in specific ways.</p></p>
