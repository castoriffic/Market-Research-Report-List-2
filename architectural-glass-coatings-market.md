<p><p>Architectural Glass Coatings are divided into two main types, namely Energy Saving Thermal Insulation Coatings and Functional Coatings. Energy-saving thermal insulation coatings are designed to reduce heat transfer through windows, resulting in reduced energy consumption for heating or cooling purposes. On the other hand, functional coatings provide added functionality to glass surfaces, such as anti-glare, self-cleaning, or anti-condensation properties. Both types of coatings enhance the performance and efficiency of glass in architectural applications, contributing to energy savings and improved functionality.</p></p>
