<p><p>Sympathomimetic agents are drugs that mimic or stimulate the effects of the sympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for the "fight or flight" response. These agents primarily act on adrenergic receptors in the body, increasing the release or inhibiting the reuptake of norepinephrine, dopamine, and other catecholamines. They are commonly used in the treatment of various medical conditions like hypotension, asthma, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), and nasal congestion.</p><p>The global sympathomimetic agents market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years. Several factors contribute to this growth, including the increasing prevalence of respiratory disorders, rising global geriatric population, and the growing incidence of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) in children and adults.
