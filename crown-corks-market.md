<p><p>Crown corks, commonly used as bottle caps, come in two market types: tin-free and tin-plated. Tin-free crown corks are made without a layer of tin on their surface. They are often preferred for their cost-effectiveness and eco-friendliness. Tin-plated crown corks, on the other hand, have a layer of tin applied to their surface. This plating offers enhanced corrosion resistance, making them suitable for products that might be exposed to moisture or acidic environments. Overall, both market types provide different advantages depending on the specific needs and requirements of the beverage industry.</p></p>
