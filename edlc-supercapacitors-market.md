<p><p>EDLC (Electric Double-Layer Capacitor) Supercapacitors are energy storage devices that consist of two electrodes separated by an electrolyte. These capacitors store electrical energy by forming an electrical double layer at the electrode-electrolyte interface, which enables high charge storage. There are two main types of EDLC supercapacitors: Double Layer capacitors, which store energy through the physical separation of charges at the electrode-solution interface, and Pseudocapacitors, which store energy through fast and reversible faradaic reactions. Both types have different material compositions and characteristics, allowing for diverse applications in various industries.</p></p>
