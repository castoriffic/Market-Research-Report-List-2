<p><p>Biological wastewater treatment is a process that uses microorganisms to remove contaminants from wastewater. There are two main types of biological wastewater treatment: aerobic and anaerobic. Aerobic treatment involves the use of oxygen by microorganisms to break down organic matter, while anaerobic treatment occurs in the absence of oxygen. Both methods are used in the wastewater treatment market, with aerobic treatment being more common. Aerobic treatment is effective in removing a wide range of contaminants, while anaerobic treatment is mostly used for high-strength wastewater or to produce biogas.</p></p>
