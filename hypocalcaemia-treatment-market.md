<p><p>Hypocalcaemia refers to the condition of having an abnormally low level of calcium in the blood. It can occur due to various factors such as vitamin D deficiency, hypoparathyroidism, or renal failure. Hypocalcaemia treatment aims to raise the levels of calcium in the blood to a normal range.</p><p>The primary treatment for hypocalcaemia involves calcium replacement therapy. This therapy typically involves intravenous administration of calcium in the form of calcium gluconate or calcium chloride. In some cases, the underlying cause of hypocalcaemia also needs to be addressed. For instance, if vitamin D deficiency is the cause, vitamin D supplements may be prescribed.</p><p>The hypocalcaemia treatment market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.8% during the forecast period.
