<p><p>The Intracranial Pressure Monitoring Device market can be classified into two types: invasive and noninvasive. Invasive devices involve the insertion of a probe into the skull to directly measure the pressure inside the brain. These devices are typically more accurate but come with certain risks. On the other hand, noninvasive devices can measure intracranial pressure indirectly through techniques like transcranial Doppler ultrasonography. Although noninvasive devices are safer and more convenient, they may not provide the same level of accuracy as invasive devices.</p></p>
