<p><p>A spray dryer absorber is a type of flue gas desulfurization (FGD) technology commonly used in power plants and chemical industries to remove acidic gases, such as sulfur dioxide (SO2), from flue gases. It involves spraying a slurry of absorbent material, usually lime or limestone, into the flue gas stream in a spray dryer chamber. The absorber material reacts with the acidic gases, neutralizing them and creating solid particles that are collected in downstream equipment.</p><p>The spray dryer absorber market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for efficient and clean power generation. Stringent environmental regulations and norms regarding emission control have propelled the adoption of FGD technologies, including spray dryer absorbers, across various industries. These technologies help reduce environmental pollution and improve air quality.</p><p>The market growth is also driven by the growth of coal-fired power plants and industrial processing sectors, especially in emerging economies. <p><p>The Spray Dryer Absorber market consists of two main types: Rotary Atomizer and Two-Fluid Nozzle. Rotary Atomizer is a type of spray dryer that uses a rotating wheel to generate fine droplets of liquid. It is widely used in industries due to its high efficiency and ability to handle various liquid feedstocks. Two-Fluid Nozzle, on the other hand, utilizes the principle of atomization through the interaction of two different fluids, usually a liquid and a gas. It offers precise control over droplet size and is commonly used in applications requiring narrow particle size distribution.</p></p>
