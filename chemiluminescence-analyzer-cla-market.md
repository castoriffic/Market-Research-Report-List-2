<p><p>A Chemiluminescence Analyzer (CLA) is a type of instrument used in scientific research and analysis to measure the amount of chemiluminescence emitted by a sample. CLAs can be categorized into two main types: benchtop and floor-standing. </p><p>Benchtop CLAs are compact and designed for use on laboratory benches or small workspaces. They are ideal for individual researchers or small laboratories that require accurate and precise measurements in a limited space.</p><p>Floor-standing CLAs, on the other hand, are larger and more robust instruments that are suitable for high-volume testing or larger laboratories. They offer enhanced capabilities and are often used by research institutions or industries that require continuous and heavy-duty analysis.</p><p>Overall, both benchtop and floor-standing CLAs serve the purpose of measuring chemiluminescence but differ in size, capacity, and suitability for different laboratory settings and specifications.</p></p>
