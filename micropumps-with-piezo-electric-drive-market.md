<p><p>Micropumps with piezo-electric drive are innovative devices used to pump small amounts of liquids or gases. The market is classified into two types: piezo-electric liquid micropumps and piezo-electric gas micropumps. Piezo-electric liquid micropumps use piezoelectric materials to generate vibrations, forcing the liquid to flow. These micropumps find applications in medical devices, lab equipment, and inkjet printers. On the other hand, piezo-electric gas micropumps utilize similar principles to move gases efficiently, making them suitable for gas sampling, gas analysis, and fuel cells. Both markets cater to various industries demanding precise and controllable fluid or gas movement.</p></p>
