<p><p>The bladder stimulator market is divided into implantable and non-implantable types. Implantable bladder stimulators are surgically implanted devices that deliver electrical impulses to the nerves that control bladder function, helping to improve bladder control and reduce urinary incontinence. Non-implantable bladder stimulators, on the other hand, are external devices that use electrical or magnetic stimulation to modulate nerve activity and treat urinary retention or incontinence. Both types aim to provide individuals with a more efficient and effective bladder control mechanism, but differ in terms of their placement and method of stimulation.</p></p>
