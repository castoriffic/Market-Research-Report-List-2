<p><p>The purified human proteins market comprises two main types: native purified human proteins and recombinant purified human proteins. Native purified human proteins are derived directly from human tissues or fluids using various purification techniques. These proteins maintain their natural structure and biological activity, making them valuable for research and diagnostic purposes. On the other hand, recombinant purified human proteins are produced through genetic engineering techniques, where specific genes are inserted into host cells, allowing for the production of large quantities of targeted proteins. These recombinant proteins have various applications in therapeutics and drug development.</p></p>
