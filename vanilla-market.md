<p><p>The vanilla market consists of four main types: bean, extract, powder, and paste. Vanilla beans are the whole dried pods, prized for their intense flavor and aroma. Vanilla extract is made by steeping the beans in alcohol and offers a more convenient form for culinary use. Vanilla powder is simply ground vanilla beans, often mixed with a small amount of sugar or other additives. Lastly, vanilla paste combines the essence of vanilla extract with the flavorful seeds, offering a rich and concentrated form of vanilla for baking and cooking purposes.</p></p>
