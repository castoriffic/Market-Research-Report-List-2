Polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cells (PEMFC) are a popular type, known for their high power density, quick startup time, and ability to operate at low temperatures. They use a solid polymer membrane as the electrolyte. Solid oxide fuel cells (SOFC), on the other hand, operate at high temperatures and are more suitable for larger vessels due to their durability and ability to efficiently use various fuels. Both types have potential in the marine industry's shift towards cleaner and sustainable energy sources.</p></p>
