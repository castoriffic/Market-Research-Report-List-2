<p><p>Vacuum Insulating Glass (VIG) is a type of glazing that consists of two or more glass panes separated by a vacuum gap. The edges of the glass panes are hermetically sealed to prevent air and moisture from entering the gap. The vacuum acts as an insulating barrier, reducing thermal conductivity and enhancing energy efficiency in buildings.</p><p>VIG offers various advantages over conventional double or triple glazing systems. It provides better thermal insulation, reducing heat loss and energy consumption for heating or cooling. It also offers improved sound insulation, as the vacuum acts as a barrier to sound transmission. Additionally, VIG eliminates condensation and reduces the potential for mold growth in the airspace.</p><p>The market analysis shows positive growth prospects for the Vacuum Insulating Glass Market. The increasing emphasis on energy-efficient and sustainable buildings is driving the demand for VIG in the construction industry. <p><p>Vacuum Insulating Glass (VIG) is available in two different types - tempered VIG and non-tempered VIG. Tempered VIG refers to glass that undergoes a heating and cooling process, known as tempering, to increase its strength and resistance to breakage. This type of VIG is suitable for applications where safety is a concern, as it shatters into small, rounded pieces when broken. Non-tempered VIG, on the other hand, does not go through the tempering process and may break into sharp, jagged pieces. Its use is generally limited to applications where safety is not a major consideration.</p></p>
