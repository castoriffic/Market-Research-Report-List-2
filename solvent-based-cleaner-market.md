<p><p>Solvent-based cleaners can be categorized into different market types based on the type of solvents used in their composition. </p><p>1. Oxygenated solvents: These cleaners contain solvents like alcohols, esters, or ketones. They are effective in removing a wide range of contaminants and are often used in industries like paints and coatings, automotive, or electronics.</p><p>2. Halogenated solvents: These cleaners contain solvents containing elements like chlorine or fluorine. They are known for their strong solvency power and are commonly used in industries such as degreasing, metal cleaning, or dry cleaning.</p><p>3. Hydrocarbon solvents: These cleaners contain petroleum-derived solvents such as aliphatic hydrocarbons or aromatic hydrocarbons. They are versatile, cost-effective, and widely used in various applications including paint thinning, adhesive removal, or parts cleaning.</p><p>4. Other market types: Apart from the above categories, solvent-based cleaners can also include specialty solvents tailored for specific purposes, such as precision cleaning, electronics cleaning, or medical devices cleaning. These cleaners often have unique formulation requirements to meet specific industry needs.</p></p>
