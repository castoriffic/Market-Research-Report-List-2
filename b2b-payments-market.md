<p><p>The B2B payments market can be categorized into two types: cross-boundary and non-cross-boundary. Cross-boundary refers to transactions that occur between businesses located in different countries or regions. These payments involve foreign currencies and may be subject to international regulations and exchange rate fluctuations. On the other hand, non-cross-boundary transactions are those that take place within a single country or region, involving businesses located in the same economic jurisdiction. These payments are typically conducted in the local currency and are subject to domestic regulations and policies.</p></p>
