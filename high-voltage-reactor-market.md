<p><ul><li>Series Reactor</li><li>Shunt Reactor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The high voltage reactor market comprises two main types: series reactors and shunt reactors. Series reactors are connected in series with the transmission line to improve system stability, limit short-circuit currents, and reduce voltage fluctuations. On the other hand, shunt reactors are connected in parallel to the transmission line to compensate for inductive reactance, maintain voltage levels at transmission points, and stabilize the power system. Both types of reactors play critical roles in regulating power flow and ensuring efficient and reliable electricity transmission in high voltage systems.</p></p>
