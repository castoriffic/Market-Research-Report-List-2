<p><p>Photovoltaic power generation systems can be categorized into three types: 1) Stand-alone systems, also known as off-grid systems, operate independently and are not connected to the utility grid. They are commonly used in remote areas or for individual households. 2) Grid-connected systems are connected to the utility grid, allowing excess electricity to be fed back into the grid and providing electrical power during periods of low generation. These systems are widely used in urban areas. 3) Distributed systems involve smaller-scale photovoltaic installations that are connected to the local distribution network, typically serving a specific building or facility.</p></p>
