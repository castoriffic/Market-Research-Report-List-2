<p><p>Circulators and isolators are key components used in RF (Radio Frequency) and microwave systems. They are passive devices that help regulate and control the flow of RF signals within a system to prevent interference and system damage.</p><p>Circulators are three-port devices that allow the flow of RF signals in one direction, while isolating signals in the reverse direction. They are commonly used in radar systems, satellite communication systems, telecommunication networks, and test and measurement equipment. Circulators provide protection to sensitive components by isolating reflected signals and preventing them from reaching the source.</p><p>Isolators, on the other hand, are two-port devices that allow the flow of RF signals in one direction while attenuating signals in the reverse direction. They are primarily used to protect RF microwave sources (such as amplifiers) from reflected power, which can cause instability and damage to the source.</p><p>The market for circulators and isolators is expected to witness steady growth in the coming years.
