<p><p>Powered paramotors, also known as powered paragliders, come in two main types: 2-stroke and 4-stroke paramotors. A 2-stroke paramotor operates using a two-stroke combustion engine, which ignites a fuel-air mixture to generate power. These paramotors tend to be lighter and more compact, offering greater agility and convenience for pilots. On the other hand, 4-stroke paramotors feature a four-stroke combustion engine that offers improved fuel efficiency and reduced emissions. This type of paramotor is generally known for its smoother and quieter operation, appealing to pilots seeking a more refined flying experience.</p></p>
