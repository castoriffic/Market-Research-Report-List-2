<p><p>MET inhibitor drugs refer to a class of pharmaceuticals that target the MET receptor, which plays a role in cell growth and survival. There are various types of MET inhibitors available in the market. C-Met biological inhibitors and small molecule C-Met inhibitors are drugs that directly bind to the MET receptor and inhibit its activity. C-Met antagonist antibodies and HGF antagonist antibodies are drugs that block the interaction between the MET receptor and its ligand, while Kringle variant antagonists compete with the MET receptor for binding sites. These drugs are used in the treatment of cancer and other diseases where the MET pathway is dysfunctional.</p></p>
