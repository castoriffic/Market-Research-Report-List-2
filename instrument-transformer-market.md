<p><p>Instrument transformers are devices used to measure or monitor electrical quantities in power systems. Current transformers (CTs) are designed to step down high current values to a manageable level for measurement or protection purposes. Voltage transformers (VTs) are used to reduce high voltage levels to appropriate levels for measurement or protective relaying. These two types are widely used in the instrument transformer market. Other types of instrument transformers, such as potential transformers, capacitive voltage transformers, and combined current/voltage transformers, may also be used for specific applications.</p></p>
