<p><p>Bone marrow transplantation is a medical procedure used to treat various diseases and disorders. There are two main types of bone marrow transplantation: allogeneic and autologous. Allogeneic bone marrow transplant involves the transfer of healthy bone marrow cells from a compatible donor to the recipient. This procedure is commonly used for patients with leukemia or lymphoma. On the other hand, autologous bone marrow transplant involves collecting and storing a patient's own healthy bone marrow cells before undergoing high-dose chemotherapy or radiation therapy. These stored cells are then reintroduced to the patient's body to replace damaged or diseased marrow. This type of transplant is often used for patients with solid tumors or certain blood disorders.</p></p>
