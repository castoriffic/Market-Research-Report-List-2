<p><strong>The Central Vascular Access Devices Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC)</li><li>Tunneled Catheters</li><li>Non Tunneled Catheters</li><li>Implanted Ports</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Central vascular access devices (CVADs) are medical devices used to access the circulatory system for various purposes. Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC) are inserted into a peripheral vein and advanced into a larger central vein. Tunneled catheters are long-term access devices that are inserted through a small incision and tunneled under the skin to a central vein. Non-tunneled catheters are temporary devices that are inserted directly into a central vein. Implantable ports are devices placed under the skin, allowing repeated access to a central vein for long-term use.</p></p>
