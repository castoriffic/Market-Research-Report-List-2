<p><p>Chemotherapy-induced neutropenia (CIN) is a common side effect of chemotherapy, characterized by a decrease in the number of neutrophils, a type of white blood cell that plays a crucial role in fighting infections. CIN can increase the risk of infections, delay chemotherapy cycles, and even lead to dose reductions or treatment discontinuations.</p><p>The treatment for CIN involves the administration of granulocyte colony-stimulating factors (G-CSFs), which stimulate the production of neutrophils. Filgrastim and pegfilgrastim are the most commonly used G-CSFs in CIN treatment. These drugs help reduce the risk of infection, hospitalizations, and delays in chemotherapy cycles.</p><p>The chemotherapy-induced neutropenia treatment market is expected to experience substantial growth in the coming years. Factors such as the increasing incidence of cancer worldwide, growing adoption of chemotherapy, and rising awareness about CIN and its management are driving the market growth.
