<p><ul><li>Internal Fixation Devices</li><li>External Fixed Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical fixation devices refer to devices used to stabilize and immobilize fractured bones during the healing process. Internal fixation devices are surgically implanted to hold bones in place, such as plates, screws, and rods. They are preferred for complex fractures and allow for faster healing. External fixation devices are placed externally, using pins or wires, to hold bones in place. They are used for temporary stabilization or with open fractures where there is a risk of infection. Both types of devices play a crucial role in promoting proper healing and recovering mobility in patients.</p></p>
