<p><p>In Situ Hybridization (ISH) is a technique used in molecular biology to visualize and localize specific nucleic acid sequences within cells or tissues. It involves hybridizing a labeled nucleic acid probe to the target sequence of interest, which is then detected using various methods such as fluorescence, chromogenic, or radioactive detection. This technique is widely used in research laboratories, diagnostic laboratories, and pharmaceutical companies for various applications including gene expression analysis, genetic mapping, and detection of specific DNA or RNA sequences.</p><p>The In Situ Hybridization Service Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
