<p><p>The Semiconductor IP Cores market is segmented into three types: soft cores, hard cores, and firm cores. Soft cores refer to intellectual property (IP) designs that can be easily modified or customized by the user according to their requirements. Hard cores, on the other hand, are fixed and non-modifiable designs that are typically pre-verified and fabricated. Lastly, firm cores provide the flexibility of soft cores along with some fixed functionality. These different core types cater to varying needs and preferences of semiconductor manufacturers and designers in the market.</p></p>
