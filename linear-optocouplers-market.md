<p><p>Linear optocouplers are electronic devices used to transfer signals between two electrically isolated circuits. There are various types of linear optocouplers available in the market. Photovoltaic-output optocouplers generate an electrical current proportional to the input light. Transistor-output optocouplers use a phototransistor to amplify and control the output current. Triac-output optocouplers are designed for AC power applications, controlling the power to the load. Thyristor-output optocouplers use a thyristor to switch AC or DC signals. IC-output optocouplers integrate multiple components on a single chip. Lastly, there are other types that incorporate unique features and capabilities for different applications.</p></p>
