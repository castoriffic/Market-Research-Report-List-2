<p><p>Electrolysis is a method used to produce solid caustic soda, a strong base widely used in various industries. Two common types of electrolysis methods used for producing solid caustic soda are the Ion Exchange Membrane Method and the Diaphragm Electrolysis Method. In the Ion Exchange Membrane Method, a special membrane separates the anode and cathode, allowing only positive ions to pass through and forming solid caustic soda. In the Diaphragm Electrolysis Method, a porous diaphragm separates the anode and cathode, preventing the mixing of chlorine gas and hydrogen gas, thus producing solid caustic soda.</p></p>
