<p><p>Concrete admixture construction chemicals are substances added to concrete during the mixing process to enhance its properties. Mineral admixtures are derived from natural materials and include substances like fly ash, silica fume, and slag, which improve the concrete's strength and durability. Chemical admixtures, on the other hand, are synthetic substances such as plasticizers, superplasticizers, and accelerators that modify the concrete's workability, setting time, and strength. Both types of admixtures play vital roles in enhancing the performance, workability, and sustainability of concrete in construction projects.</p></p>
